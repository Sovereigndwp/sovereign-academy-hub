Why your node is the only vote you can verify

By the end of this lesson

You can explain what a full node checks, run one verification against a block you choose, and say precisely what "don't trust, verify" costs and buys.

Rules without rulers

Every bitcoin node holds a full copy of the rulebook and checks every block against it — 21 million coins, valid signatures, no double spends. There is no appeal process and no customer support, because there is nothing to appeal: a block either follows the rules or it does not exist.

When you run your own node, nobody summarizes the state of the network for you. You compute it.

Verify this yourself

Ask your node for the current supply. No block explorer, no website — your own hardware answers:

$ bitcoin-cli gettxoutsetinfo
"total_amount": 19,876,543.21

takes ~2 min on a home node · the number is the audit

What verification costs

Disk space, sync time, and the discipline to check instead of ask. That is the whole bill. What it buys: you never have to believe anyone — an exchange, a developer, this academy — about what the rules are or whether your money follows them.

Caution

A wallet connected to someone else's node trusts that node's answers. Fine for coffee money; not fine for savings. Lesson 7.4 covers pointing your wallet at your own node.

Knowledge check

Your node rejects a block that a major exchange's explorer shows as confirmed. Who is right about your money?

The exchange — it has more users
Your node — its rules are the only ones you verified
Whichever confirms first
