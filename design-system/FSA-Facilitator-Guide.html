MODULE 04 · FACILITATOR GUIDE
◆Session plan · 4 of 8

Make a budget you can keep

Time
60 min
Group size
6–20
Materials
4-A, pencils
Prep
Copy p.12–15
By the end of this session

Every participant has listed real must-pay expenses, sorted must-pay from can-wait, and written one set-aside plan in their own words.

0:00–0:10
Open with the scenario, not the worksheet

Read Marcus's situation aloud. Ask: "What happens Friday night if there's no plan?" Take three answers. Do not correct any of them yet.

0:10–0:16
Hand out worksheet 4-A · step 1

Six minutes, working alone. Answers vary — the total matters less than naming every must-pay.

0:16–0:30
Pairs compare lists · whole-group sort

Pairs swap lists and mark anything they'd move between must-pay and can-wait. Then build the group list on the board — write it exactly as participants say it.

0:30–0:48
Steps 2–3 · the set-aside plan

Checkboxes together, reflection lines alone. If someone writes nothing, ask them to say it out loud first — then write exactly that.

0:48–1:00
Close: one sentence each

"The first thing I set aside is ___ because ___." Around the room, no passes, no comments after. End on the last voice, not yours.

Watch for

The two-week vs. monthly math trips someone every session — the sidebar reminder on worksheet p.12 is there for this. Also: participants who finish step 1 in one minute usually listed wants as must-pays. Ask about consequences, not categories.

Say it plainly

"Must-pay" not "fixed expense." "Left over" not "discretionary." The glossary is for reading, not for talking.

Timing cue

If the group sort runs past 0:35, cut the pairs debrief — never the reflection lines.

No-device rule

Everything in this session works with paper and a board. Calculators optional, never required.
